# Problem 4 – Conservation of energy

A body falls from height \(h\) without air resistance.

---

## 1. Total energy \(E(h)\)

The total mechanical energy is:

$$
E=T+U
$$

Kinetic energy:

$$
T=\frac12 mv^2
$$

Potential energy:

$$
U=mgh
$$

So:

$$
E=\frac12 mv^2+mgh
$$

Since there is no air resistance, mechanical energy is conserved.

---

## 2. Velocity as a function of height \(y\)

Suppose the body starts from rest at height \(h\).

Initial energy:

$$
E_0=mgh
$$

At height \(y\), the energy is:

$$
E=\frac12 mv^2+mgy
$$

By conservation of energy:

$$
mgh=\frac12 mv^2+mgy
$$

Subtract \(mgy\):

$$
mg(h-y)=\frac12 mv^2
$$

Multiply by 2 and divide by \(m\):

$$
2g(h-y)=v^2
$$

Thus:

$$
v(y)=\sqrt{2g(h-y)}
$$

---

## 3. Comparison with Newton's second law

From Newton's second law for free fall:

$$
a=g
$$

Using kinematics:

$$
v^2=v_0^2+2as
$$

Since \(v_0=0\) and \(s=h-y\), we get:

$$
v^2=2g(h-y)
$$

Thus:

$$
v(y)=\sqrt{2g(h-y)}
$$

This agrees with the energy result.

---

## 4. At what height does kinetic energy equal 75% of total energy?

Total energy is constant:

$$
E=mgh
$$

We want:

$$
T=0.75E
$$

So:

$$
\frac12 mv^2=0.75mgh
$$

But from energy conservation:

$$
T=mg(h-y)
$$

Thus:

$$
mg(h-y)=0.75mgh
$$

Divide by \(mg\):

$$
h-y=0.75h
$$

So:

$$
y=0.25h
$$

Therefore, the body has 75% of the total energy as kinetic energy at height:

$$
y=\frac14 h
$$

---

## Final results

Total energy:

$$
E=\frac12 mv^2+mgy
$$

Velocity as a function of height:

$$
v(y)=\sqrt{2g(h-y)}
$$

Height where kinetic energy is 75% of total energy:

$$
y=\frac14 h
$$

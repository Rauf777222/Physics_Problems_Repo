# Problem 5 – Momentum and one-dimensional head-on collision

Two bodies with masses \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) move along one straight line.  
The collision is elastic.

Let the initial velocities be \(u_1\) and \(u_2\), and the final velocities be \(v_1\) and \(v_2\).

---

## 1. Conservation of momentum

In an elastic collision, total momentum is conserved:

$$
m_1u_1+m_2u_2=m_1v_1+m_2v_2
$$

---

## 2. Conservation of kinetic energy

Since the collision is elastic, kinetic energy is also conserved:

$$
\frac12 m_1u_1^2+\frac12 m_2u_2^2=
\frac12 m_1v_1^2+\frac12 m_2v_2^2
$$

Multiplying by 2:

$$
m_1u_1^2+m_2u_2^2=m_1v_1^2+m_2v_2^2
$$

---

## 3. Velocities after the collision

The standard formulas for a one-dimensional elastic collision are:

$$
v_1=\frac{m_1-m_2}{m_1+m_2}u_1+\frac{2m_2}{m_1+m_2}u_2
$$

$$
v_2=\frac{2m_1}{m_1+m_2}u_1+\frac{m_2-m_1}{m_1+m_2}u_2
$$

---

## 4. Special case \(m_1=m_2\)

If \(m_1=m_2\), then:

$$
v_1=u_2
$$

$$
v_2=u_1
$$

So the bodies exchange velocities.

---

## 5. Limit case \(m_2\gg m_1\)

If the second mass is much larger than the first one, then approximately:

$$
v_1\approx -u_1+2u_2
$$

and

$$
v_2\approx u_2
$$

If the heavy body was initially at rest (\(u_2=0\)), then:

$$
v_1\approx -u_1
$$

$$
v_2\approx 0
$$

So the light body bounces back, while the heavy body barely moves.

---

## 6. Physical interpretation

- Momentum conservation expresses the balance of motion.
- Energy conservation means the collision is perfectly elastic.
- Equal masses exchange velocities.
- A very heavy body behaves almost like a wall.

---

## Final results

Momentum conservation:

$$
m_1u_1+m_2u_2=m_1v_1+m_2v_2
$$

Energy conservation:

$$
m_1u_1^2+m_2u_2^2=m_1v_1^2+m_2v_2^2
$$

Final velocities:

$$
v_1=\frac{m_1-m_2}{m_1+m_2}u_1+\frac{2m_2}{m_1+m_2}u_2
$$

$$
v_2=\frac{2m_1}{m_1+m_2}u_1+\frac{m_2-m_1}{m_1+m_2}u_2
$$

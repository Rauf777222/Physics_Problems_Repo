# Problem 1 – Harmonic motion: motion parameters

The harmonic motion is given by

$$
x(t)=A\cos(\omega t+\varphi)
$$

---

## 1. Period and frequency

For harmonic motion:

$$
\omega = 2\pi f
$$

Therefore:

$$
f=\frac{\omega}{2\pi}
$$

and the period is

$$
T=\frac{1}{f}=\frac{2\pi}{\omega}
$$

---

## 2. Maximum velocity and maximum acceleration

Differentiate position:

$$
v(t)=\dot x(t)=-A\omega \sin(\omega t+\varphi)
$$

So the maximum velocity is

$$
v_{\max}=A\omega
$$

Differentiate again:

$$
a(t)=\ddot x(t)=-A\omega^2\cos(\omega t+\varphi)
$$

So the maximum acceleration is

$$
a_{\max}=A\omega^2
$$

---

## 3. For \(A=0.2\ \text{m}\), \(f=2\ \text{Hz}\)

### Angular frequency

$$
\omega=2\pi f=2\pi\cdot 2=4\pi\ \text{rad/s}
$$

### Maximum velocity

$$
v_{\max}=A\omega=0.2\cdot 4\pi=0.8\pi\ \text{m/s}
$$

Approximate value:

$$
v_{\max}\approx 2.51\ \text{m/s}
$$

### Maximum acceleration

$$
a_{\max}=A\omega^2=0.2(4\pi)^2
$$

$$
a_{\max}=0.2\cdot 16\pi^2=3.2\pi^2\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

Approximate value:

$$
a_{\max}\approx 31.58\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

## Final results

$$
T=\frac{2\pi}{\omega}
$$

$$
f=\frac{\omega}{2\pi}
$$

$$
v_{\max}=A\omega
$$

$$
a_{\max}=A\omega^2
$$

For \(A=0.2\), \(f=2\):

$$
\omega=4\pi\ \text{rad/s}
$$

$$
v_{\max}=0.8\pi\approx 2.51\ \text{m/s}
$$

$$
a_{\max}=3.2\pi^2\approx 31.58\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

# Problem 3 – Harmonic wave

Given wave:

$$
y(x,t)=A\sin(kx-\omega t)
$$

---

## 1. Wavelength

The wave number is related to wavelength by:

$$
k=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}
$$

Thus:

$$
\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{k}
$$

---

## 2. Phase velocity

The phase velocity is:

$$
v_p=\frac{\omega}{k}
$$

---

## 3. For \(k=4\pi\), \(\omega=20\pi\)

### Wavelength

$$
\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{4\pi}=\frac12
$$

So:

$$
\lambda=0.5
$$

### Phase velocity

$$
v_p=\frac{20\pi}{4\pi}=5
$$

So:

$$
v_p=5
$$

---

## 4. Does the point \(x=\lambda\) oscillate in phase with the point \(x=0\)?

At \(x=0\):

$$
y(0,t)=A\sin(-\omega t)
$$

At \(x=\lambda\):

$$
y(\lambda,t)=A\sin(k\lambda-\omega t)
$$

Since

$$
k\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\cdot \lambda=2\pi
$$

we get:

$$
y(\lambda,t)=A\sin(2\pi-\omega t)
$$

Because sine is \(2\pi\)-periodic:

$$
\sin(2\pi-\omega t)=\sin(-\omega t)
$$

Thus the oscillations are in phase.

---

## Final results

$$
\lambda=\frac{2\pi}{k}
$$

$$
v_p=\frac{\omega}{k}
$$

For \(k=4\pi\), \(\omega=20\pi\):

$$
\lambda=0.5
$$

$$
v_p=5
$$

The points \(x=0\) and \(x=\lambda\) oscillate in phase.
